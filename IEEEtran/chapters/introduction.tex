\IEEEraisesectionheading{\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}}

\IEEEPARstart{S}{tress} can have severe consequences on the human body ranging from depression, anxiety, and social withdrawal to sleep deprivation and obesity. In addition, stress directly influences the cardiovascular system \cite{mcewen2017neurobiology}. A wide variety of methods exist to monitor these cardiovascular signals and to track stress levels. The measurement of pulse waves is called plethysmography. Photoplethysmography (PPG) utilizes light reflectance to detect physiological changes. A traditional method for PPG is contact-based sensors, such as pulse oximeters \cite{verkruysse2008remote}. Remote Photoplethysmography (rPPG) uses standard video cameras to monitor the skin color and filter for subtle skin-color variations that are caused by flowing blood. Based on this information, the heart rate can be calculated \cite{verkruysse2008remote}. These measurements can be directly reported back to the user using biofeedback. Biofeedback describes a therapeutic concept in which a user receives real-time information about their human body, such as respiration or heart rate. This data is typically made accessible through an output device, such as a visual display. Alternatively, the information can be translated into different forms, such as auditory signals. The primary goal of biofeedback is to make these biological processes visibel. This enables the user to gain conscious awareness of his own body \cite{giggins2013biofeedback}.

The objective of this paper is to evaluate a camera-based stress-tracking system combined with musical biofeedback. The goal is to analyze whether such a system can be utilized to lower stress levels and regulate consciousness.

In the following chapters, each individual component of this system is investigated. The second chapter focuses on the physiological reactions of the body when stress is perceived and its corresponding impact on the heart. The third chapter examines how stress can be measured using a remote camera. The fourth chapter goes into detail regarding why simple music listening without biofeedback is insignificant. The fifth chapter explains strategies for converting these measurements into sound. Finally, the sixth and seventh chapters explain how biofeedback leads to regeneration and the regulation of consciousness.
